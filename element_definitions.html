B1 Assignment: Element Definitions

h1
Level 1 heading. Usually the title of your page. Can have subheadings from 2-6. The higher the number the smaller the font.
<h1>This is my important flashy title in bold</h1>
p
Used to break blocks of texts into easier to read paragraphs.
<p>Bla Bla I'm an important paragraph</p>
b
Used to make stuff bold. Can be one word in a paragraph.
<b>BOOM</b>
i
Used to italicize stuff.
<i>Enterprise<i> was captained by Jonathan Archer.
sup
Used for superscript. As in E=MC2.
E=MC<sup>2</sup>
sub
Used for subscript. As in H2O.
HC<sub>2</sub>O.
br
Used for line breaks. As in:
1
2
3
1</br>2</br>3</br>
hr
Creates breaks between themes.
<hr>I was talking about intergalactic travel. Now i'm talking about how awesome cheese is.</hr>
strong
Used to show that it's content is important. Not to be confused with bold even they they look the same. Not a style choice an added info choice.
<strong>Do not<strong> touch the moving blade.
em
Used to emphasize subtle changes that can change meaning. Not to be confused with italicizing even they they look the same. Not a style choice an added info choice.
<em>Bleh</em>
blockquote
Used for longer quotes.
<blockquote>Insert freaking long quote here.</blockquote>
q
Used for shorter quotes.
<q>Be who you are and say what you feel because those who mind don't matter and those who matter don't mind.</q>
abbr
Used to clarify an abbreviation or acronym.
<abbr title="Professor">Prof</abbr> Oak.
